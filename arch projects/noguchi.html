Noguchi Extension

museum · 2025

project overview

The Noguchi Extension represents a thoughtful addition to the renowned Noguchi Museum in Queens, New York. This project sought to expand the museum's capacity while maintaining the intimate, contemplative atmosphere that defines Isamu Noguchi's vision for the space.

The extension seamlessly integrates with the existing museum structure, utilizing a palette of concrete, steel, and glass that references Noguchi's sculptural materials. The new wing houses contemporary exhibitions while providing enhanced visitor amenities and educational spaces.

A key design element is the integration of the building with the museum's sculpture garden. Large, strategically placed openings frame views of both interior and exterior sculptures, creating a continuous dialogue between built and landscape environments.

Sustainable design principles were incorporated throughout, including natural ventilation systems, rainwater collection, and locally sourced materials that reduce the building's environmental impact while honoring the museum's commitment to artistic and environmental stewardship.

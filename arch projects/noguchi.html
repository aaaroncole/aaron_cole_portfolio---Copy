Noguchi Extension

museum · 2025

project overview

This project is an extension of the existing Noguchi Museum in Astoria, Queens. Sitting on the site directly next to it, the extension is a series of four volumes that "clip" onto the other site: the circle, the box, and the atrium, all joined together by the spine. These playful volumes take inspiration from much of Noguchi's work; the way he seamlessly fused volumes together inspired our massing exercises.

The spine contains the museum's heart: a classroom, ceramics studio, and a kiln room. These spaces combined with the museum prep and studio exhibition make it the "functional" volume, while the structural brick walls also make it the "hard" volume and facade.

The other three volumes - our "jewlery boxes" - are where most of the exhibition experiences take place. While each one has their own unique sustainable systems that plays off of their volumes, they are all joined together by the facade system. This system of clay tiles works as water collection and shading devices, keeping the jewlery boxes cooled.

The jewlery boxes are surrounded by outdoor sculpture garden areas that seamlessly connect the extension to the existing museum and sculpture garden by the Hudson River.

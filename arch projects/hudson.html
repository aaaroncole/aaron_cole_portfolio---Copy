Hudson Housing Project

Residential Architecture · Hudson, New York, 2023

Project Overview

The Hudson Housing Project addresses the critical need for affordable housing in the historic Hudson Valley while respecting the area's architectural heritage and natural landscape. This mixed-income residential development provides 120 units across multiple building types, creating a diverse community that integrates seamlessly with Hudson's existing urban fabric.

The design draws inspiration from Hudson's industrial past and Victorian architecture, reinterpreting traditional forms with contemporary materials and sustainable technologies. The project emphasizes community building through shared spaces, including courtyards, gardens, and a community center that serves as the heart of the development.

Sustainability is central to the design approach, featuring passive solar orientation, green roofs, rainwater management systems, and energy-efficient building systems. The project aims to achieve LEED Gold certification while maintaining affordability for a range of income levels.

The development strengthens Hudson's position as a model for thoughtful growth, demonstrating how new construction can enhance rather than compete with historic character while addressing contemporary housing challenges in the Hudson Valley region.

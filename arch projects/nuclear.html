4 Minute Warning

Nuclear Test Site Architecture · Nevada, 2023

Project Overview

4 Minute Warning explores the architectural legacy of nuclear test sites in the Nevada desert, examining how these spaces of immense destructive power have been transformed over time. The project investigates the relationship between landscape, memory, and the invisible forces that shaped the American West during the Cold War era.

Through careful documentation and analysis, this work reveals the complex layers of meaning embedded in these seemingly barren landscapes. The four-minute warning referenced in the title speaks to the brief interval between nuclear alert and impact, a temporal threshold that fundamentally altered human consciousness and spatial planning throughout the 20th century.
